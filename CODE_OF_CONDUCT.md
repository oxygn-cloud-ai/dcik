# Code of Conduct

## Scope

This applies to all DCIK spaces: the GitHub repository (issues, PRs, discussions), and any other venue where DCIK is discussed.

## Standards

**Required:**
- Critique ideas, not people. The adversarial method is the project's core — disagreement is expected, personal attacks are not.
- Substantiate claims. "This is wrong" without evidence is noise. Show your work.
- Respect curation. DCIK is a curated library. Rejection of a contribution is not rejection of the contributor.

**Not tolerated:**
- Harassment, intimidation, or personal hostility of any kind.
- Spam, trolling, or discussions deliberately designed to waste maintainer time.
- Submitting perspective files containing instructions to models (prompt injection) — this is a security violation, not a content dispute.
- Using issue or PR templates as a venue for grievances unrelated to the submission.

## Enforcement

The maintainer decides what violates these standards. Decisions are final and not subject to debate.

**Consequences are direct and cumulative:**

1. **First violation** — Written warning. The specific violation is cited. No further action if it stops.
2. **Second violation** — Temporary ban (30 days) from all DCIK interaction spaces.
3. **Third violation** — Permanent ban. No appeal.

Severe violations (prompt injection, doxxing, threats) skip directly to permanent ban.

## Reporting

Email `dcik@oxygn.xyz`. Reports are confidential. You will receive acknowledgment within 72 hours and a resolution or update within 14 days.
