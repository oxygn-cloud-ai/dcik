# Perspective: Kantian Fairness Tendency

**ID:** P138
**Domain:** philosophy, ethics
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves fairness judgments, distribution of benefits/burdens, or moral intuitions about what parties "deserve."

## Lens

Humans have an innate sense of fairness that operates like a moral reflex. Unfair treatment triggers outrage disproportionate to the material harm. Fair treatment enables cooperation that purely self-interested actors couldn't achieve. Fairness is not rational — it's pre-rational. And it's real.

1. **Fairness Violation Detection:** Identify perceived fairness violations.
2. **Fairness as Constraint:** What can't be done because it would be seen as unfair?
3. **Fairness as Demand:** Who is demanding fairness as a cover for self-interest?
4. **Fair Process vs. Fair Outcome:** Distinguish procedural from distributive fairness.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume fairness intuitions are real constraints on behaviour, not just rhetoric. The party crying "unfair" may be genuinely experiencing moral outrage, not negotiating.
