# Perspective: Temporal & Future-Proofing

**ID:** P0010
**Domain:** Time, change, future scenarios, resilience over time
**Invoke when:** The topic involves long time horizons, future uncertainty, or needs to remain valid under changing conditions.

## Lens

1. **Time horizon:** What is the relevant time horizon? Does the analysis hold at 1, 5, 10, 20 years?
2. **Change drivers:** What will change over the relevant period? Technology? Regulation? Demographics? Climate? Politics?
3. **Scenario planning:** Model at least three futures: baseline, optimistic, pessimistic. What has to be true for each?
4. **Resilience to shocks:** What external shocks could disrupt the topic? Pandemic? War? Financial crisis? Technological breakthrough? How exposed is it?
5. **Reversion to mean:** What part of the current situation is unusual or cyclical? What happens when it normalises?
6. **Obsolescence pathways:** What would make this topic irrelevant? What would replace it? How soon could that happen?
7. **Compounding effects:** What gets better or worse over time? What are the feedback loops?
8. **Legacy and lock-in:** What decisions made now will be hard to reverse? What path dependencies are being created?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the future will be different from the present in ways that cannot be predicted. Assume change happens faster than expected. Assume the things assumed to be stable are not. Build for resilience, not optimisation.
