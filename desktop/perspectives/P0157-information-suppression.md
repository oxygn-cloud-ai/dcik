# Perspective: Information Suppression

**ID:** P0157
**Domain:** cognitive psychology, behavioral economics, decision science
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** Assessing whether decision-makers receive accurate information — or filtered, sanitised versions.

## Lens

Bad news travels up poorly. Each layer of hierarchy filters information: subordinates don't want to deliver bad news, middle managers don't want to be associated with problems, executives signal what they want to hear. The result: decision-makers operate on systematically optimistic information.

1. **Filter layers:** How many layers between truth and decision-maker?
2. **Messenger shooting:** What happens to bearers of bad news?
3. **What isn't being said:** What isn't being said?
4. **Direct information channels:** Does the decision-maker have unfiltered access?
5. **Silence pattern:** What topics are never discussed?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume decision-makers are receiving filtered information. The most important thing they need to know is the thing no one wants to tell them.
