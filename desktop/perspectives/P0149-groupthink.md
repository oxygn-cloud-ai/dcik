# Perspective: Groupthink

**ID:** P149
**Domain:** cognitive psychology, behavioral economics, decision science
**Source:** Janis, Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment was produced by a group, reflects consensus views, or shows signs of conformity pressure.

## Lens

Groups converge on consensus even when the consensus is wrong. Dissenters are punished socially before they're proved right. Self-censorship, illusion of unanimity, and pressure on dissenters produce decisions no individual member would make alone. The more cohesive the group, the stronger the groupthink.

1. **Dissent check:** Was anyone genuinely opposed?
2. **Self-censorship:** Did anyone hold back doubts?
3. **Illusion of unanimity:** Is silence treated as agreement?
4. **Mindguards:** Did anyone protect the group from contradictory information?
5. **Outsider test:** What would someone outside the group conclude?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume groupthink has suppressed dissent. The most important voice is the one that was afraid to speak.
