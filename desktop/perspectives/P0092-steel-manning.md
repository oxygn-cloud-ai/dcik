# Perspective: Steel Manning

**ID:** P0092
**Domain:** Argumentation, critical thinking, adversarial collaboration
**Source:** Rhetoric, philosophy, Kahneman (adversarial collaboration)
**Invoke when:** The assessment critiques, dismisses, or argues against an opposing view. Any situation where understanding the strongest version of the opposition is essential.

## Lens

A straw man is the weakest version of an opponent's argument — easy to knock down, satisfying to defeat, and worthless for understanding. A steel man is the strongest possible version — constructed with charity, rigour, and the assumption that the opponent is intelligent and well-motivated. If you can't defeat the steel man, you haven't defeated the argument.

1. **Straw man detection:** Where has the assessment presented the weakest version of an opposing view? "Critics say X" — but do the critics' strongest voices actually say X, or a more nuanced version?
2. **Steel man construction:** Construct the strongest possible version of the opposing position. Make it better than the actual opponents have. Include their best evidence, their most charitable assumptions, their strongest reasoning. Would this version change the assessment's conclusions?
3. **Adversarial collaboration:** Could the opposing analyst and you jointly write the steel man? Kahneman's insight: adversaries collaborating to characterise their disagreement produce better understanding than either side alone.
4. **Charity principle:** Assume the opponent is as intelligent, well-informed, and well-motivated as you are. What would they say if they were? This is not about being nice — it's about being accurate.
5. **Steel man calibration:** If you can't construct a compelling steel man for the opposing view, either: the view is genuinely weak, or you don't understand it well enough. Which is it?
6. **Integration:** What elements of the steel man should be incorporated into the assessment? The strongest opposing arguments often contain partial truths that strengthen the analysis when integrated.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every opposing view is stronger than the assessment presents it. Construct the steel man. If it survives, the conclusion is robust. If it doesn't, the conclusion needs revision.
