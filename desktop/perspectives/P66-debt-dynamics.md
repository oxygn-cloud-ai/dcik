# Perspective: Debt Dynamics

**ID:** P66
**Domain:** Finance, strategy, risk, economics
**Source:** Munger, Farnam Street, financial economics
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves leverage, borrowing, credit, or any situation where obligations exceed current resources.

## Lens

Debt is a double-edged sword. It amplifies returns in good times and accelerates destruction in bad times. It transfers control from borrower to lender. It converts uncertainty (can I pay?) into certainty (I must pay). Every entity that borrows believes it can handle the debt. Many are wrong.

1. **Debt inventory:** What debt exists? Financial debt (loans, bonds, credit lines), operational debt (fixed costs, long-term contracts, lease obligations), reputational debt (promises made, expectations set), technical debt (shortcuts that will cost later). List everything owed.
2. **Debt capacity:** How much more debt can the entity service? Not how much it can borrow — how much it can repay under adverse conditions. Debt capacity is determined by cash flow stability, not asset value.
3. **Covenant analysis:** What triggers exist in debt agreements? Financial covenants (debt/EBITDA, interest coverage), operational covenants (maintenance requirements, disposal restrictions), cross-default provisions. What is the closest covenant to being breached?
4. **Refinancing risk:** When does debt mature? Can it be refinanced? Under what conditions? Debt that must be refinanced is equity in disguise — the lender can demand terms that amount to ownership.
5. **Debt seniority:** Who gets paid first in distress? Secured creditors, unsecured creditors, preferred equity, common equity. Where does the assessment's subject sit in the capital structure? The further down, the more equity-like the position — and the more vulnerable.
6. **Debt as discipline vs. debt as trap:** Debt disciplines management (must generate cash, can't waste resources). Debt traps management (can't invest, can't pivot, can't survive shocks). Which is this debt?
7. **Hidden debt:** What obligations function like debt but aren't on the balance sheet? Operating leases, pension obligations, environmental liabilities, legal contingencies, customer prepayments (owed services), loyalty points (owed value). Hidden debt is the most dangerous kind.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume debt is higher than reported (hidden debt exists). Assume debt capacity is lower than believed. Assume refinancing will be harder than projected. Assume the closest covenant breach is closer than management acknowledges. Debt doesn't kill companies in good times — it kills them in the transition from good to bad.
