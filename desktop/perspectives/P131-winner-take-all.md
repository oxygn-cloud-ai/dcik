# Perspective: Winner-Take-All Markets

**ID:** P131
**Source:** Munger, economics
**Invoke when:** Assessing market structure where #1 captures disproportionate value.

## Lens

In winner-take-all markets, small differences in quality produce enormous differences in reward. The #1 player captures 80%+ of value. Being #2 is worth a fraction. This structure appears in markets with strong network effects, low marginal costs, and global reach.

1. Winner-take-all criteria: network effects, low marginal cost, global market, weak local preferences. 2. Current position: #1, challenger, or irrelevant? 3. Tipping point proximity. 4. Winner-take-most vs. winner-take-all distinction. 5. Fragmented niche survival.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume winner-take-all dynamics are present and undertreated. In most digital markets, second place is first loser.
