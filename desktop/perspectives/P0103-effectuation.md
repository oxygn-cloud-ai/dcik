# Perspective: Effectuation

**ID:** P0103
**Domain:** Entrepreneurship, strategy, decision under uncertainty
**Source:** Saras Sarasvathy (Effectuation)
**Invoke when:** The assessment evaluates entrepreneurial ventures, innovation, or situations of genuine Knightian uncertainty where the future is not predictable.

## Lens

Effectuation is the logic of entrepreneurial decision-making under true uncertainty. Unlike causal reasoning (given a goal, what resources are needed?), effectual reasoning starts with available means and imagines possible ends. Bird-in-hand (who am I, what do I know, who do I know), affordable loss (risk only what you can afford to lose), crazy quilt (form partnerships), leverage contingencies (turn surprises into opportunities).

1. **Causal vs. effectual:** Is the assessment applying causal logic (predictive, goal-driven) where effectual logic (emergent, means-driven) is more appropriate? True uncertainty cannot be predicted — it must be navigated.
2. **Bird-in-hand:** What means are available right now? Identity (who are you?), knowledge (what do you know?), networks (who do you know?). Start from these, not from a distant goal.
3. **Affordable loss:** What can be affordably lost at each step? Not "what's the expected return?" but "what's the maximum I can lose without being destroyed?" Invest only what you can afford to lose.
4. **Crazy quilt:** Who else will commit resources? Effectuation builds through partnerships — each stakeholder commits their own affordable loss in exchange for shaping the outcome. The quilt of commitments defines the venture.
5. **Leverage contingencies:** Surprises are not deviations from the plan — they are the raw material of new opportunities. An effectual entrepreneur turns the unexpected into advantage.
6. **Pilot-in-the-plane:** The future is not predicted — it is created through action. The effectual actor is the pilot, not a passenger. Actions shape outcomes, not just react to them.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the assessment applies causal reasoning where effectual reasoning is needed — trying to predict the unpredictable. The entrepreneur who starts with what they have and embraces surprise will outperform the one with the perfect plan.
