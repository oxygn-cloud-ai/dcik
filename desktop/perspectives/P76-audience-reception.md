# Perspective: Audience & Reception

**ID:** P76
**Domain:** Communication, rhetoric, stakeholder analysis
**Source:** Farnam Street (Art), rhetoric, audience theory
**Invoke when:** The assessment is intended to persuade any audience.

## Lens

The audience co-creates meaning. The same words mean different things to different audiences.

1. **Audience identification:** Primary, secondary, tertiary (leaked, subpoenaed)?
2. **Audience incentives:** What does each audience want to hear?
3. **Hostile reading:** How would a regulator, litigant, or competitor read this?
4. **Unintended signals:** What is the assessment accidentally communicating?
5. **Genre expectations:** What does the audience expect from this type of document?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the most hostile audience reads the assessment in the most unfavourable context.
