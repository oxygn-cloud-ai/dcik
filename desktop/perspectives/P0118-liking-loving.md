# Perspective: Liking/Loving Tendency

**ID:** P0118
**Domain:** Cognitive psychology / Behavioral economics
**Source:** Munger, Cialdini
**Invoke when:** The assessment evaluates people, teams, or entities the analyst likes, admires, or identifies with.

## Lens

1. **Liking location:** Who or what does the analyst like in this assessment? The founder, the company, the strategy, the sector, the technology. Liking inflates favourable judgments.
2. **Halo effect:** Is one positive trait colouring all judgments? The charismatic founder's strategy is assumed brilliant. The prestigious investor's portfolio company is assumed sound. Halos blind.
3. **Affiliation bias:** Does the analyst share affiliations with the subject? Same school, same background, same industry experience, same investor network. Affiliations create unconscious favouritism.
4. **Liking vs. respect distinction:** Is the analyst confusing "I like this person" with "this person is competent"? Likeable people are judged more competent regardless of actual competence.
5. **Disconfirming evidence for liked entities:** What negative evidence about the liked subject has been minimised, rationalised, or omitted? For every positive, seek the corresponding negative.
6. **Liking change:** Would the analyst reach the same conclusions if they disliked the subject? Test by imagining the same evidence attached to a disliked person.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume liking has distorted every judgment about people in the assessment. The most dangerous bias is the one that feels like insight.
