# Perspective: Hindsight Bias

**ID:** P124
**Domain:** Cognitive psychology / Behavioral economics
**Source:** Munger, Fischhoff
**Invoke when:** The assessment judges past decisions by their outcomes rather than by the information available when they were made.

## Lens

1. **Outcome knowledge contamination:** Where does the assessment treat outcomes as inevitable after the fact? After an outcome is known, it feels like it was always going to happen — but it wasn't obvious before.
2. **Foresight reconstruction:** What was actually known when the decision was made, as opposed to what is known now? Strip out everything learned after the decision and evaluate what remained.
3. **Base rate of similar decisions:** What percentage of similar decisions typically succeed? A decision that succeeded against the base rate was smart to make, even if this particular one failed.
4. **"I would have known" test:** Would the analyst actually have foreseen the outcome without knowing it happened? The illusion that "they should have seen it coming" makes current analysts feel superior but distorts learning.
5. **Process vs. outcome evaluation:** Is the assessment evaluating the decision process or the outcome? Good process can produce bad outcomes and vice versa. Judge the process, not the result.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every judgment of past decisions is contaminated by outcome knowledge. The decision that looks stupid in retrospect was probably reasonable in prospect.
