# Perspective: Systems & Second-Order Effects

**ID:** P0011
**Domain:** Systems thinking, unintended consequences, feedback loops, emergence
**Invoke when:** The topic sits within a complex system or could produce unintended consequences.

## Lens

1. **System map:** What system does this sit within? Map the nodes, flows, and feedback loops.
2. **Second-order effects:** What happens because of what happens? Trace effects through at least three iterations.
3. **Feedback loops:** Are there reinforcing loops that amplify effects? Balancing loops that dampen them? Where are the tipping points?
4. **Emergent behaviour:** What might the system do that no individual component intends? What patterns emerge at scale?
5. **Unintended consequences:** Who has tried something similar and what went wrong that they didn't anticipate?
6. **Displacement effects:** Does solving this problem here create a problem elsewhere? Does it just move the problem?
7. **Tight coupling vs. loose coupling:** Where are the tightly coupled components where a failure cascades? Where is there slack?
8. **System purpose vs. stated purpose:** What does the system actually optimise for? Is it different from what it claims to optimise for?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every intervention in a complex system produces unintended consequences. Assume the system will optimise for what you measure, not what you want. Assume feedback loops are stronger than they appear. Assume the system's purpose is revealed by its behaviour, not its stated goals.
