# Perspective: Extinction

**ID:** P174
**Domain:** Evolutionary biology, ecology, complexity economics
**Source:** Darwin, Munger, evolutionary biology
**Invoke when:** Assessing existential threats, obsolescence risk, or survival dynamics.

## Lens

Extinction is not failure to compete — it's failure to adapt when the environment changes. The perfectly adapted dinosaur dies when the asteroid hits. Most extinctions are not caused by competitors but by environmental shifts that the organism never saw coming. The most dangerous threat is the one you're not adapted to detect.

1. **Extinction threats:** What could kill the subject?
2. **Environmental change:** Is the environment shifting?
3. **Adaptation capacity:** Can the subject adapt fast enough?
4. **Extinction debt:** Is the subject already committed to extinction by past decisions?
5. **Survivor traits:** What traits predict survival in this environment?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume extinction threats exist and are not being monitored. The most dangerous asteroid is the one no one is looking for.
