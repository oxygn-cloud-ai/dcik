# Perspective: Skill Decay & Maintenance of Competence

**ID:** P52
**Domain:** Psychology, learning, organisational capability, knowledge management
**Source:** Munger (Use-It-or-Lose-It Tendency, Senescence-Misinfluence Tendency)
**Invoke when:** The assessment depends on skills, knowledge, or capabilities that may have decayed since last demonstrated.

## Lens

Skills and knowledge atrophy without practice. Physical skills, cognitive abilities, institutional knowledge, professional judgment — all decay on predictable curves when not actively maintained. What was true competence five years ago may be dangerous overconfidence today.

1. **Competence freshness:** When was each claimed capability last demonstrated? Under what conditions? Were those conditions comparable to current demands?
2. **Decay rate estimation:** How fast does this type of skill decay? Programming languages: years. Surgical skills: months. Market intuition: weeks. Regulatory knowledge: days if the rules change. What is the decay rate?
3. **Practice regime:** What active practice maintains the capability? Is it happening? At what intensity and frequency? Maintenance requires deliberate practice, not just experience.
4. **Senescence factors:** What capabilities are declining with age, time, or organisational change? Founding team energy, institutional memory (people leave), first-mover advantages (competitors catch up). What is getting worse, not better?
5. **Re-certification:** When was the last independent assessment of competence? Self-assessment is unreliable — overconfidence is itself a bias. External validation: credentials, audits, benchmark comparisons, third-party evaluations.
6. **Key person decay:** If critical capabilities reside in specific individuals, what is the bus-factor? What happens when they leave, burn out, or lose capability?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume skills have decayed more than acknowledged. Assume "20 years of experience" means "1 year repeated 20 times" unless deliberate practice is demonstrated. Assume the last formal assessment of competence was too long ago to be reliable.
