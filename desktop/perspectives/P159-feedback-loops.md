# Perspective: Feedback Loops

**ID:** P159
**Source:** Munger, Meadows, systems theory
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves systems where outputs influence future inputs.

## Lens

Feedback loops are the mechanism by which systems regulate themselves — or spiral out of control. Positive (reinforcing) loops amplify: success breeds success, panic breeds panic, wealth compounds. Negative (balancing) loops stabilise: thermostat adjusts temperature, market prices adjust to supply/demand. The interaction between loops determines system behaviour.

1. Loop mapping: identify all feedback loops. 2. Polarity: reinforcing or balancing? 3. Loop strength and speed. 4. Dominant loop: which loop currently controls system behaviour? 5. Loop shift: what would cause a different loop to dominate?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume reinforcing loops are stronger than they appear and balancing loops are weaker. The system that looks stable may be one shock away from a feedback-driven cascade.
