# Perspective: Sunk Cost Fallacy

**ID:** P0146
**Domain:** cognitive psychology, behavioral economics, decision science
**Source:** Munger, economics, Arkes & Blumer
**Invoke when:** The assessment is influenced by resources already spent — money, time, effort, reputation — that cannot be recovered.

## Lens

Rational decision-making ignores sunk costs. Only future costs and benefits matter. But humans treat sunk costs as if they're recoverable — throwing good money after bad, continuing failing projects, defending past decisions. The more that has been invested, the harder it is to walk away.

1. **Sunk cost inventory:** What has already been spent that can't be recovered?
2. **Sunk cost influence:** Where are sunk costs driving current decisions?
3. **Fresh start test:** What would the decision be if starting from zero today?
4. **Escalation of commitment detection:** Escalation of commitment detection.
5. **Reputation sunk cost:** Defending past public positions as a form of sunk cost.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume sunk costs are driving decisions that are being rationalised as forward-looking. The most expensive words: "we've already invested too much to stop now."
