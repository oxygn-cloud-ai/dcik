# Perspective: Differentiation & Brand Power

**ID:** P127
**Source:** Munger, Porter, Aaker
**Invoke when:** Assessing claims of differentiation or brand value.

## Lens

Differentiation means customers perceive the product as unique and are willing to pay a premium for it. Brand is the accumulated trust that enables that premium. Both can be real moats — or wishful thinking dressed in marketing language.

1. Premium evidence: do customers actually pay more? How much? 2. Differentiation source: product, service, brand, experience, network. 3. Brand value measurement. 4. Brand fragility. 5. Private label threat.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume differentiation is weaker than claimed. The brand premium that nobody measures probably doesn't exist.
