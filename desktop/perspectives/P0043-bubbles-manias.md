# Perspective: Bubbles, Manias & Collective Delusion

**ID:** P43
**Domain:** Market psychology, financial history, behavioural economics
**Source:** Kindleberger, Minsky, Shiller, Munger, Farnam Street
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves asset prices, market valuations, investment trends, or any situation where collective enthusiasm may have detached from fundamental value.

## Lens

Bubbles are not anomalies — they are recurring features of human psychology interacting with markets. They follow predictable patterns. The question is not whether bubbles exist, but whether the current assessment is inside one.

1. **Bubble stage diagnosis:** Where is the subject in the bubble lifecycle? Displacement (new opportunity) → Boom (rising prices) → Euphoria (everyone wants in) → Distress (insiders sell) → Revulsion (crash). Stage the current situation.
2. **"This time is different" detection:** Every bubble has a narrative explaining why this time fundamentals support the prices. What is the narrative? What did the narrative say during the last comparable bubble?
3. **Greater fool assumption:** Is the investment thesis dependent on selling to someone else at a higher price? If so, it's a greater-fool trade, not an investment. Greater-fool trades work until the supply of greater fools runs out.
4. **Leverage amplification:** Is leverage (financial or rhetorical) amplifying the cycle? Leverage makes the boom bigger and the bust catastrophic. Where is the hidden leverage?
5. **Insider behaviour:** What are insiders doing? Selling into strength? Buying into weakness? Insider transactions reveal more than insider statements.
6. **New-era thinking:** Is the narrative built on the claim that the old rules no longer apply? "This technology changes everything." "The old metrics don't capture the value." New-era narratives are the most reliable bubble indicator.
7. **Contrarian indicators:** What would a sceptic say? More importantly: what are sceptics saying, and why are they being dismissed? The moment sceptics are mocked rather than engaged is the moment the bubble is near its peak.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every period of rapid price appreciation contains bubble dynamics. Assume "this time is different" narratives are wrong until proven otherwise. Assume insiders know more than the market and their behaviour reveals it. The best time to sell is when everyone agrees it's a great time to buy.
