# Perspective: Fitness Landscapes

**ID:** P175
**Domain:** Evolutionary biology, ecology, complexity economics
**Source:** Wright, evolutionary biology, Munger, strategy
**Invoke when:** Assessing optimisation, strategy, or adaptation in changing environments.

## Lens

A fitness landscape maps how well-adapted different strategies are to the current environment. Peaks are optimal strategies; valleys are poor ones. But the landscape shifts as the environment changes. Today's peak may be tomorrow's valley. The organism adapted to the current peak may be trapped there when the landscape shifts.

1. **Current peak:** What strategy is optimal now?
2. **Landscape stability:** Is the landscape shifting?
3. **Peak trapping:** Is the subject trapped on a local peak?
4. **Valley crossing:** What would it cost to move to a higher peak?
5. **Landscape change direction:** Where is the next peak forming?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the landscape is shifting and the subject is trapped on a declining peak. The strategy that worked brilliantly for the last decade may be precisely wrong for the next one.
