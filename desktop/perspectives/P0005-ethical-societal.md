# Perspective: Ethical & Societal

**ID:** P05
**Domain:** Ethics, social impact, externalities, fairness, public good
**Invoke when:** The topic has societal implications, ethical dimensions, or affects non-parties.

## Lens

1. **Who is harmed?** Identify every party who could be negatively affected, including those with no voice in the decision.
2. **Externalities:** What costs does this impose on third parties? Are they priced in?
3. **Fairness:** Would a reasonable disinterested observer consider the outcome fair? If not, why not?
4. **Power asymmetry:** Who has power and who doesn't? Is the structure exploitative of information or power gaps?
5. **Long-term societal impact:** What does this do to society if it succeeds at scale? If everyone did this?
6. **Regulatory and reputational risk from ethical failures:** Even if legal, is it right? What happens when the press covers it?
7. **Intergenerational equity:** Does this benefit current participants at the expense of future ones?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the topic, if successful, will be scrutinised by the most hostile possible journalist, regulator, and public. Assume any ethical shortcut will eventually be exposed. Assume "everyone does it" is not a defence.
