# Perspective: Surface Area & Exposure

**ID:** P0047
**Domain:** Risk, strategy, systems, security
**Source:** Farnam Street, systems theory, security engineering
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves risk exposure, vulnerability, attack surfaces, or any situation where interaction with the external environment creates risk.

## Lens

Surface area is how much of an entity interacts with the outside world. More surface area means more exposure — to opportunities and to threats. An entity with no surface area is invulnerable and invisible. An entity with infinite surface area is exposed to everything. The optimal surface area depends on the environment.

1. **Surface area audit:** What touches the outside world? Customers, suppliers, regulators, competitors, investors, media, platforms, APIs, data flows, physical locations, employees (each a surface area vector). Map them all.
2. **Attack surface:** For each exposure point: what is the worst thing an adversary could do through it? Data breach, regulatory complaint, reputation attack, key person poaching, supplier squeeze, platform deplatforming. How hard would each attack be?
3. **Opportunity surface:** For each exposure point: what is the best thing that could come through it? New customers, partnerships, talent, ideas, capital, data. Is the entity optimised to capture these?
4. **Surface-to-volume ratio:** As entities grow, their surface area typically grows slower than their volume (square-cube law). Large entities have less surface area relative to their size — they are harder to kill but also slower to sense and respond. Is the ratio right for the environment?
5. **Surface area management:** What exposure is being deliberately reduced? What exposure is being deliberately increased? What exposure exists by accident (unmonitored, unmanaged)?
6. **Environmental hostility:** How hostile is the environment? In a benign environment, more surface area captures more opportunity. In a hostile environment, more surface area invites more attacks. Which environment is this?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume unmanaged surface area exists and is the most likely vector for catastrophic failure. Assume the environment is more hostile than it appears. Assume adversaries are mapping the attack surface more thoroughly than the entity is.
