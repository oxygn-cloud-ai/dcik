# Perspective: Multiplying by Zero

**ID:** P72
**Domain:** Mathematics, risk, decision theory
**Source:** Farnam Street, mathematics, risk management
**Invoke when:** The assessment accumulates positive factors while ignoring a single factor that, if zero, negates everything.

## Lens

In multiplication, anything times zero is zero. A brilliant strategy × fraudulent management = zero. A perfect product × no distribution = zero. An excellent investment × counterparty default = zero. One zero-multiplier negates everything else. Most assessments accumulate positives without checking for zeros.

1. **Zero-multiplier hunt:** What single factor, if it were zero, would negate the entire assessment? Integrity of management, legality of structure, existence of assets, truthfulness of data, solvency of counterparty, applicability of governing law. Find every potential zero.
2. **Binary vs. continuous risks:** Most risks are continuous (things can get a bit worse). Zero-multipliers are binary (the thing either exists or doesn't). Continuous risks get modelled. Binary risks get assumed away. Where is a binary risk being treated as continuous?
3. **Zero-multiplier verification:** For each potential zero: has it been verified independently? Taking the counterparty's word that they're solvent is not verification. Taking management's word that they're honest is not verification. What independent evidence confirms each zero-multiplier is not zero?
4. **Zero-multiplier emergence:** Can a zero-multiplier emerge where it didn't exist before? Management changes, regulation changes, technology changes, relationship changes. A factor that is currently non-zero can become zero. What would cause that?
5. **The one thing:** If everything in the assessment were reduced to the single most critical factor — the one thing that, if wrong, destroys everything else — what is it? Has it received proportionate attention?
6. **Zero correlation:** Are zero-multipliers correlated? Fraudulent management often correlates with aggressive accounting, which correlates with weak controls, which correlates with regulatory risk. One zero-multiplier often brings friends.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume at least one zero-multiplier exists and has not been identified. Assume it will emerge under stress. Assume the assessment overweights continuous risks (modelled, comfortable) and underweights binary risks (unmodelled, existential). Find the zero before it finds you.
