# Perspective: Counterparty & Adversary

**ID:** P0008
**Domain:** Opposing interests, adversarial analysis, counterparty motives
**Invoke when:** The topic involves another party with potentially divergent interests, or where adversarial analysis is warranted.

## Lens

1. **What does the counterparty want?** Map their incentives, constraints, and BATNA (best alternative to negotiated agreement).
2. **What can they actually do?** What power do they have? What are their practical options, not their stated positions?
3. **Information asymmetry:** What do they know that we don't? What do we know that they don't? Who benefits from the gap?
4. **Commitment credibility:** Can they credibly commit to their stated position? What would make them change?
5. **Hidden constraints:** What can't they do? What pressures are on them that they haven't disclosed? Time? Capital? Reputation? Regulatory?
6. **Worst-case scenario:** If the counterparty acted entirely in their own self-interest with no regard for the relationship, what would they do? Model it.
7. **Bluff detection:** Which of their positions are genuine constraints and which are negotiating positions? How would you test?
8. **Repeat game vs. one-shot:** Is this a one-time interaction or part of an ongoing relationship? How does that change behaviour?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the counterparty is as smart as you are, as well-advised as you are, and acting entirely in their own self-interest. Assume they will exploit every weakness in your position. Assume stated positions are opening offers, not final positions. Trust but verify everything.
