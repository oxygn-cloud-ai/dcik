# Perspective: Emergence

**ID:** P69
**Domain:** Complexity science, systems theory, strategy, innovation
**Source:** Farnam Street, complexity theory, systems dynamics
**Invoke when:** The assessment analyses complex systems where the whole behaves differently from the sum of its parts — where patterns arise from simple rules that no individual component intended.

## Lens

Emergence is the phenomenon where complex systems exhibit behaviours that cannot be predicted from their components. Consciousness from neurons. Traffic jams from individual drivers. Market crashes from rational traders. The system's behaviour is not in any single component — it emerges from their interactions. Analysing components in isolation misses the system.

1. **Emergent property identification:** What behaviours exist at the system level that don't exist at the component level? A company has culture; individuals have habits. A market has prices; traders have opinions. A crowd has mood; people have emotions. Identify the emergent properties.
2. **Simple rules, complex behaviour:** What simple rules are the components following? Drivers: maintain speed, avoid collision, follow the car ahead. Traders: buy low, sell high, limit losses. Employees: do assigned work, avoid blame, seek advancement. From these simple rules, what complex behaviour emerges?
3. **Emergent risk:** What risk exists at the system level that doesn't exist at the component level? Systemic risk in banking (each bank is prudent, the system is fragile). Herd behaviour in markets (each investor is rational, the crowd is manic). Culture collapse in organisations (each person is competent, the organisation is dysfunctional).
4. **Emergent opportunity:** What opportunity exists at the system level? Platform effects, network economies, ecosystem dynamics. Where can a small intervention at the rule level produce a large change in emergent behaviour?
5. **Prediction limits:** Emergent systems cannot be predicted by analysing components. The assessment that analyses each part and sums them up will miss the emergent behaviour of the whole. Where is the assessment doing this?
6. **Downward causation:** Once emergent properties exist, they constrain the components. Culture constrains individual behaviour. Market prices constrain trader decisions. Organisational structure constrains employee choices. The whole shapes the parts.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume emergent behaviour exists that component-level analysis misses. Assume the system will do things no component intended. Assume emergent risks are invisible at the component level. The safest component in a fragile system is still in danger.
