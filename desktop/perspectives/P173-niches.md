# Perspective: Niches

**ID:** P173
**Domain:** Evolutionary biology, ecology, complexity economics
**Source:** Ecology, Munger, Farnam Street
**Invoke when:** Assessing market positioning, specialisation, or competitive strategy.

## Lens

A niche is a specialised position where particular traits thrive and generalists struggle. The niche player doesn't compete with everyone — they dominate a narrow domain where their specific adaptations give advantage. Niches can be highly profitable and defensible — or fragile if the niche disappears.

1. **Niche identification:** What is the subject's niche?
2. **Niche defensibility:** Can others enter?
3. **Niche size:** How large is the addressable niche?
4. **Niche fragility:** What would make this niche disappear?
5. **Niche expansion:** Can the niche grow or must the subject find new niches?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the niche is narrower than claimed and less defensible than hoped. The specialist dominates until the niche disappears.
