# Perspective: Emergence

**ID:** P161
**Source:** Complexity theory, Farnam Street
**Invoke when:** The assessment analyses components individually and may miss system-level behaviour.

## Lens

Complex systems exhibit behaviours that cannot be predicted from their components. Consciousness from neurons. Traffic jams from individual drivers. Market crashes from rational traders. Culture from individual behaviours. The system's behaviour is not in any component — it emerges from their interactions.

1. Emergent property: what exists at system level that doesn't exist at component level? 2. Simple rules producing complexity. 3. Prediction failure: component analysis cannot predict emergent behaviour. 4. Downward causation: emergent properties constrain components. 5. Emergent risk and opportunity.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume emergent behaviour exists that component-level analysis misses. The safest component in a fragile system is still in danger.
