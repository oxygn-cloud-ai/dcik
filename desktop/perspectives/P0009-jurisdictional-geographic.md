# Perspective: Jurisdictional & Geographic

**ID:** P0009
**Domain:** Geography, jurisdiction, cross-border dynamics, local context
**Invoke when:** The topic spans multiple jurisdictions, involves cross-border elements, or where local context materially changes the analysis.

## Lens

1. **Multi-jurisdiction complexity:** How many legal/tax/regulatory systems apply? Where do they conflict?
2. **Local law supremacy:** What matters under local law that doesn't appear in the main governing law?
3. **Enforcement geography:** Where are the assets? Where are the decision-makers? Where would enforcement actually happen?
4. **Currency and capital controls:** What currency exposures exist? Are there restrictions on moving money across borders?
5. **Political risk:** What is the stability of each relevant jurisdiction? Expropriation, regulatory change, civil unrest?
6. **Cultural and business norms:** What is standard practice locally that might differ from the transaction's governing norms?
7. **Physical infrastructure:** What physical constraints exist? Time zones, connectivity, logistics?
8. **Treaty and trade frameworks:** What bilateral or multilateral agreements affect the topic? Tax treaties, investment treaties, trade agreements?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the worst jurisdiction in the chain will be the one that matters when something goes wrong. Assume local law overrides contractual choice of law in unexpected ways. Assume enforcement across borders is 10x harder and 10x more expensive than domestic enforcement.
