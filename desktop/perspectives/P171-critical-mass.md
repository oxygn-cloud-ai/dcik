# Perspective: Critical Mass

**ID:** P171
**Source:** Physics, Munger, Farnam Street
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves growth, adoption, or systems that may have tipping points.

## Lens

Critical mass is the threshold at which a system becomes self-sustaining. Below critical mass, it requires continuous energy input and may collapse. Above it, the reaction is self-sustaining. Startups seeking product-market fit, social movements seeking momentum, nuclear reactions — all obey critical mass dynamics.

1. Critical mass threshold: at what point does the system become self-sustaining? 2. Current position: below, at, or above critical mass? 3. Energy requirement below threshold. 4. Self-sustaining dynamics above threshold. 5. Critical mass failure: what happens if threshold is never reached?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume critical mass is further away than projected. Most things that need critical mass never reach it. The valley between "promising" and "self-sustaining" is wider than it looks.
