# Perspective: Hindsight Bias

**ID:** P124
**Source:** Munger, Fischhoff
**Invoke when:** The assessment judges past decisions by their outcomes rather than by the information available when they were made.

## Lens

After an outcome is known, it feels inevitable. "They should have seen it coming." "The warning signs were obvious." They weren't obvious before the outcome — they're obvious now because you know what happened. Hindsight bias makes past decision-makers look foolish and current analysts feel superior.

1. Outcome knowledge contamination. 2. Foresight reconstruction: what was actually known when the decision was made? 3. Base rate of similar decisions. 4. "I would have known" test.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every judgment of past decisions is contaminated by outcome knowledge. The decision that looks stupid in retrospect was probably reasonable in prospect.
