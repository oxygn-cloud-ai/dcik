# deep-DCIK — Accept the $24M acquisition offer, or stay independent?

*Method: six strengthened perspectives, each run as an INDEPENDENT analysis producing its own estimate + artifact, then aggregated (median where they bear on the same quantity), with disagreements surfaced. Not one voice restated six ways.*

## Aggregation & disagreement (the signal)
- **On "is 6× a floor to press against":** P0008 (62%) and P0018 (effective price well under 6×) agree it's pressable; P0012 is the dissent — a 28% chance the low bid reflects adverse private info, i.e. 6× might be *fair-to-generous*. **Aggregate: press, but diligence motive first, because the 28% tail is the one that inverts the decision.**
- **On "should we even sell":** P0013 + P0015 jointly flag that ~45% of the impulse is founder-liquidity/anchoring, not company value. This is a *distinct* finding from the valuation lenses — it says the real instrument may be a **founder secondary**, not a company sale. LITE-style integrated reasoning tends to fold this into "depends on founder risk tolerance"; the independent bias/premise lenses make it a named, weighted quantity.
- **On the crux probability:** median of the estimates bearing on "renegotiate/stay beats effective accept" (P0002 55%, P0008 62% pressable, P0018 haircut) ≈ **57%**, MEDIUM confidence.

## Decision
**Do not sign this version. Run a 3–4 week diligence-and-negotiation process. Confidence: HIGH that accepting as-is is a mistake; ~57% that renegotiating-or-staying beats the effective offer, gated on the acquirer's motive.**
- The **single fact that most changes the answer** is the acquirer's motive (P0008/P0012): if defensive/roadmap → press hard toward 8–10× or walk; the 28% adverse-info tail (P0012) is the one scenario where 6× is genuinely good — resolve it first.
- The **second, independent** lever most analyses miss: ~45% of this decision is really about *founder liquidity/anchoring*, not company value (P0013/P0015). If so, the correct instrument is a **founder secondary in the Series B**, which gets liquidity without an irreversible sale — a *different action* than "accept vs decline."
- Demand the cap-table waterfall (P0018): if founder net is largely contingent/retention, "life-changing liquidity" is false and the accept case collapses.
- **What would change the answer:** adverse acquirer motive + weak B-fundability → accept a renegotiated, de-risked deal; strategic motive + strong fundability → press or walk.

**Residual uncertainty:** motive, true deal structure, and B-fundability are all unresolved; the process resolves them in weeks. Estimates are MEDIUM-confidence and should be updated as diligence lands.
