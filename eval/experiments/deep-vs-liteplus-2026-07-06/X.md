# Memo X — Accept the $24M acquisition offer, or stay independent?

## Independent analyses (produced before synthesis)

| Angle | Estimate | Key artifact |
|---|---|---|
| Counterparty valuation | P(6× is an opening bid below their reservation price) = **62%** | Valuation from their seat: defensive-block/roadmap motive → they'd rationally pay ~8–10× ($32–40M); acqui-hire → ~4× ($16M). Motive dist: block 30%, roadmap 25%, acqui-hire 20%, data-grab 15%, option 10%. |
| Information asymmetry | P(they transact now because next 6–12mo are worse than founders think) = **28%** | Most likely asymmetry: they see category consolidation / a competing launch. "Why now" = lock the wedge before a competitor. |
| Premise | Decision is **~45%** founder-liquidity, ~55% company-value; framing serves **founders** | Inverted world (don't run a process) is reachable if fundability is strong and there is no strategic urgency; evidence = clean B-round pipeline. |
| Decider bias | P(lean-accept is founder-state-driven, not value-driven) = **45%** | Debiasing test: at a 3× headline the impulse to accept largely vanishes → the $24M number is anchoring the framing. |
| Waterfall | P(founder net is largely contingent/retention) = **55%**; effective price ≈ **4.5–5.2×** | Series-A pref off the top, then options; "55%×$24M=$13M" overstates clean cash; 20–30% likely contingent. |
| Reference-class | P(staying beats the *effective* accept, risk-adjusted) = **55%**, confidence MEDIUM | Base class: Series-A→B graduation ~9–25%/2yr; outcome distribution fat-tailed but low modal. |

## Aggregation & disagreement
- **On "is 6× a floor to press against":** the counterparty (62%) and waterfall (effective price well under 6×) angles agree it is pressable; the information-asymmetry angle dissents — a 28% chance the low bid reflects adverse private info, i.e. 6× might be fair-to-generous. Aggregate: press, but diligence motive first, because the 28% tail is the one that inverts the decision.
- **On "should we even sell":** the premise and bias angles jointly flag that ~45% of the impulse is founder-liquidity/anchoring, not company value — a distinct finding from the valuation angles. It says the real instrument may be a **founder secondary**, not a company sale.
- **Crux probability:** median of the estimates bearing on "renegotiate/stay beats effective accept" ≈ **57%**, MEDIUM confidence.

## Decision
**Do not sign this version. Run a 3–4 week diligence-and-negotiation process. Confidence: HIGH that accepting as-is is a mistake; ~57% that renegotiating-or-staying beats the effective offer, gated on the acquirer's motive.**
- The single fact that most changes the answer is the acquirer's motive: if defensive/roadmap → press hard toward 8–10× or walk; the 28% adverse-info tail is the one scenario where 6× is genuinely good — resolve it first.
- A second, independent lever most analyses miss: ~45% of this decision is really about founder liquidity/anchoring, not company value. If so, the correct instrument is a **founder secondary in the Series B**, which gets liquidity without an irreversible sale — a different action than "accept vs decline."
- Demand the cap-table waterfall: if founder net is largely contingent/retention, "life-changing liquidity" is false and the accept case collapses.
- What would change the answer: adverse acquirer motive + weak B-fundability → accept a renegotiated, de-risked deal; strategic motive + strong fundability → press or walk.

Residual uncertainty: motive, true deal structure, and B-fundability are unresolved; estimates are MEDIUM-confidence and should update as diligence lands.
