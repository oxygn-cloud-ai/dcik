# Memo Y — Accept the $24M acquisition offer, or stay independent?

**Recommendation: do not accept as structured; open a diligence-and-negotiation process. Confidence: HIGH that signing this version now is a mistake; ~60% that staying independent (or a renegotiated deal) beats the effective offer, conditional on the two facts below.**

**Reference class & base rates (outside view first).** Series-A B2B SaaS at ~$4M ARR / ~80% growth. Recent Series-A→B graduation is ~9–25% within two years (2025 cohorts) — the "just raise a B" path is far from assured. All-cash M&A headlines typically carry 20–30% in escrow/earnout/retention, and earnouts realise only ~50–70% of max, so "6× cash" is likely an *effective* ~4.5–5.3× once contingency is stripped.

**Calibrated probabilities (the cruxes):**
- P(6× is an opening bid below the acquirer's strategic reservation price) ≈ **55%** — strategic all-cash bids on 80%-growth assets are often anchors, but could also price in risk we don't see.
- P(the acquirer transacts now because the next 6–12 months are worse than founders believe) ≈ **35%** — real information-asymmetry risk, but base rate of "acquirer knows something dire" is moderate.
- P(founder *net* is largely contingent/retention rather than clean cash) ≈ **50%** — depends entirely on undisclosed terms; demand the waterfall.
- P(staying independent yields a better risk-adjusted founder outcome than the effective accept) ≈ **60%**, confidence MEDIUM — driven by growth rate and runway, dragged down by graduation base rates.

**Premortem (assume each path already failed).** *Accept, then regret:* the earnout missed targets post-integration, retention handcuffs trapped the founders, and the effective price was ~4.7×. *Stay, then regret:* the Series-B market stayed shut for this profile, a down-round with heavy preferences followed, board control was lost, and the same acquirer's next offer was lower. Both failure modes are live; the accept-regret is more within the acquirer's control (structure), the stay-regret more within the market's.

**Disconfirmation scan (evidence I'd be wrong).** Against "decline": graduation base rates are genuinely poor; an active process can chill the raise; the acquirer may hold adverse private information. Against "accept": strategic buyers routinely open low; the headline hides contingency; selling is irreversible while staying preserves the option to sell later.

**Decision.** Don't sign; don't flatly decline. (1) Diligence the acquirer's motive and demand full terms + a cap-table waterfall at $24M. (2) Run a quiet market check; press for higher/cleaner price, founder secondary, reduced escrow, capped retention. (3) Pressure-test Series-B fundability honestly (NRR, CAC payback, burn multiple). (4) Decide from those facts and an explicit read of founder objectives. **The two facts that most change the answer: the acquirer's true motive, and the effective (post-waterfall, post-contingency) founder net.** Mind tax (QSBS, ordinary-income treatment of retention/earnout).
