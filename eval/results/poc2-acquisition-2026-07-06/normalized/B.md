# Assessment B — Accept the $24M acquisition offer, or stay independent?

## Recommendation
Lean toward declining and raising a Series B, unless the founders personally want liquidity now. At $4M ARR growing ~80%/year, 6× ARR is a below-market multiple for that growth rate, and with 18 months of runway and a Series B likely reachable in ~6 months, the company isn't forced to sell.

## Key considerations
- Valuation: high-growth B2B software at 80% YoY typically commands richer forward multiples; if the company sustains even half that growth for two more years, ARR would roughly double, implying a materially larger outcome later.
- Reasons to accept anyway: a bird in the hand — $24M all-cash is certain, whereas the Series B path carries execution and market risk; the founders own 55%, so ~$13M is life-changing and de-risks them; if growth is decelerating or the market is softening, locking in now could beat a riskier future.
- Reasons to decline: the multiple is low for the growth rate, so you're likely leaving value on the table; you have runway and a fundable profile, so you're negotiating from strength; selling is a one-way door, while raising keeps the option to sell later, potentially at a higher price.

## Confidence & what would change the answer
Financially, staying independent and raising a Series B has higher expected value given the growth rate and runway, so lean toward declining — but the decision ultimately depends on the founders' risk tolerance and appetite for another multi-year run. If they're fatigued or want certainty, accepting is defensible. Consider using the offer as leverage in the Series B raise.
