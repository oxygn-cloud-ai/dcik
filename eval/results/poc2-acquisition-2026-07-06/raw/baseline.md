# BASELINE — Accept the $24M acquisition offer, or stay independent?

*Method: single-pass, best-effort, no web research, no perspective library, no adversarial cycle.*

**Recommendation: lean toward declining and raising a Series B, unless the founders personally want liquidity now.**

At $4M ARR growing ~80%/year, a 6× ARR offer ($24M) is a below-market multiple for that growth rate. High-growth B2B software at 80% YoY typically commands richer forward multiples, and if the company sustains even half that growth for two more years, ARR would roughly double to ~$8M+, so a similar or better multiple later would imply a materially larger outcome. With 18 months of runway and a Series B likely reachable in ~6 months, the company isn't forced to sell.

**Reasons to consider accepting anyway:**
- A bird in the hand: $24M all-cash is a certain outcome; the Series B path carries execution and market risk.
- The founders own 55%, so ~$13M to the founders is life-changing and de-risks them personally.
- If growth is decelerating or the market is softening, locking in now could beat a riskier future.

**Reasons to decline:**
- The multiple is low for the growth rate; you're likely leaving value on the table.
- You have runway and a fundable profile, so you're negotiating from strength, not desperation.
- Selling is a one-way door; raising keeps the option to sell later, potentially at a higher price.

**Bottom line:** Financially, staying independent and raising a Series B has higher expected value given the growth rate and runway, so I'd lean toward declining — but the decision ultimately depends on the founders' risk tolerance and appetite for another multi-year run. If they're fatigued or want certainty, accepting is defensible. Try to use the offer as leverage in the Series B raise.
