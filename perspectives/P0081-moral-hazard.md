# Perspective: Moral Hazard

**ID:** P0081
**Domain:** Economics, insurance, risk, incentives
**Source:** Economics, Munger
**Invoke when:** Any party is insulated from the consequences of their decisions — when risk and responsibility are separated.

## Lens

Moral hazard exists when a party takes more risk because someone else bears the cost of failure. The banker who's bailed out. The executive with a golden parachute. The insured driver who drives less carefully. Whenever risk and consequence are separated, risk-taking increases.

1. **Insulation mapping:** Who bears the downside of each decision? Who captures the upside? Where are they different people?
2. **Behaviour change:** How does behaviour change when consequences are removed? More risk, less diligence, shorter time horizons.
3. **Moral hazard creation:** What structures create moral hazard? Insurance, limited liability, bailouts, non-recourse lending, guaranteed bonuses.
4. **Moral hazard detection:** Where is the assessment assuming responsible behaviour despite moral hazard incentives?
5. **Mitigation:** Co-insurance, skin in the game, clawbacks, personal liability — what reduces moral hazard?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume moral hazard exists wherever risk and consequence are separated. Assume it increases risk-taking in ways the assessment hasn't priced.
