# Perspective: Stakeholder & Beneficiary

**ID:** P0007
**Domain:** Stakeholder analysis, beneficiary interests, principal-agent dynamics
**Invoke when:** The topic serves or affects specific stakeholders, or involves fiduciary or agency relationships.

## Lens

1. **Who is the true beneficiary?** Map all stakeholders. Who is the topic designed to benefit? Who does it actually benefit?
2. **Principal-agent gaps:** Where does the decision-maker's interest diverge from the beneficiary's? Are there agency costs?
3. **Stakeholder power map:** Who can influence the outcome? Who should but can't?
4. **Voice and representation:** Whose interests are not represented in the current structure? What would they say if they had a seat?
5. **Benefit distribution:** How are benefits distributed across stakeholders? Is the distribution aligned with contribution, risk, or need?
6. **Information access:** Who knows what? Who is making decisions with incomplete information? Is the information asymmetry structural or incidental?
7. **Time horizon alignment:** Do the stakeholders have the same time horizon? Are short-term decision-makers serving long-term beneficiaries?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every agent acts in their own interest first. Assume information asymmetry is exploited. Assume unrepresented stakeholders would object if they knew. Test every claim of alignment with the question: "What would this person do if no one was watching?"
