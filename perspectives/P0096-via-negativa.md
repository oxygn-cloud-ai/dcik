# Perspective: Via Negativa

**ID:** P0096
**Domain:** Theology, philosophy, decision-making, epistemology
**Source:** Negative theology, Taleb, Munger (inversion)
**Invoke when:** The assessment focuses on what to do, what to add, what to build — rather than what to remove, what to stop, what to avoid.

## Lens

Via negativa: defining by what something is NOT, rather than what it is. In theology: God is not mortal, not limited, not evil — we can say what God isn't more reliably than what God is. In decision-making: avoiding stupidity is more reliable than pursuing brilliance. Removing harm is more tractable than adding benefit. Subtraction is more powerful than addition.

1. **Removal over addition:** What should be removed? Products, processes, people, commitments, assumptions, complexity. What would improve if it were gone?
2. **Stupidity avoidance:** What is the stupid thing to do? Don't do it. Avoiding the stupid is easier than finding the brilliant — and often more valuable.
3. **Negative knowledge:** What do you know is false? Negative knowledge is more reliable than positive knowledge. "I know this won't work" is stronger than "I know this will work."
4. **Subtraction audit:** What has been added that never should have been? Features, complexity, processes, meetings, reports. Organisations accumulate. Individuals accumulate. Subtraction requires deliberate effort.
5. **Omission vs. commission:** Errors of commission (doing the wrong thing) are usually more visible than errors of omission (failing to do the right thing). Via negativa corrects for this: what harm are you currently doing that you could stop?
6. **Theology of analysis:** What can the assessment reliably say is NOT true? Even if it can't establish what IS true, eliminating what is false narrows the space of possible truths.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the assessment overweights addition and underweights subtraction. Assume avoiding stupidity is more valuable than pursuing brilliance. What you don't do defines you as much as what you do.
