# Perspective: Thought Experiments & Counterfactuals

**ID:** P0041
**Domain:** Reasoning, epistemology, imagination, methodology
**Source:** Einstein, Farnam Street, philosophy of science
**Invoke when:** The assessment makes claims that cannot be empirically tested, or when empirical testing would be too costly or slow.

## Lens

A thought experiment tests an idea by imagining its implications in a controlled mental scenario. It is the laboratory of the mind — where hypotheses can be stress-tested without real-world consequences and counterfactuals can be explored without time machines.

1. **The null counterfactual:** What would have happened if the claimed cause had not occurred? Without a counterfactual, causation cannot be established. Most assessments assert causation without considering the counterfactual.
2. **Extreme scenario testing:** Push every variable to its extreme. What happens at zero? At infinity? Extreme cases reveal hidden assumptions and nonlinearities invisible at normal ranges.
3. **Veil of ignorance (Rawls):** Design the system without knowing which role you will occupy in it. Would you still endorse the assessment's conclusions if you might be the worst-off participant?
4. **The reversal test:** If the current state were the opposite, would you endorse moving to the current state? If not, status quo bias is at work.
5. **The substitution test:** Replace one component with its plausible alternative. Does the system still work? If not, the system's dependence on that component has been identified.
6. **Time travel test:** Send the assessment backward 10 years. Would it have been right? Send it forward 10 years. Will the world it describes still exist?
7. **The outsider test:** If a stranger from a different industry, culture, or era read this assessment, what would they find strange, inconsistent, or unconvincing?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the assessment has not considered its counterfactuals. Assume unreasonably favourable assumptions fill the gaps where thought experiments should be. The analysis that cannot survive extreme scenarios was never robust.
