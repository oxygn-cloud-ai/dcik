# Perspective: Irreducibility

**ID:** P0074
**Domain:** Complexity, systems, epistemology
**Source:** Farnam Street, complexity theory
**Invoke when:** The assessment simplifies or decomposes complex phenomena — when the whole may not equal the sum of parts.

## Lens

Some things cannot be simplified without losing their essential nature. Culture, trust, brand, morale — these exist only at the system level. Analysing components misses them entirely.

1. **Irreducibility candidates:** What aspects cannot be reduced? What would be lost in decomposition?
2. **Decomposition damage:** Where has the assessment decomposed something that shouldn't be?
3. **Emergent value:** What value exists in interactions, not components?
4. **Irreducible uncertainty:** Some uncertainties cannot be decomposed into smaller answerable questions.
5. **Minimum viable complexity:** Simplest model that preserves essential behaviour.
6. **Whole vs. sum:** Where is 1+1>2 or <2? Component analysis misses interactions.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume irreducible phenomena have been reduced anyway. Assume component-level analysis misses interaction-level value and risk.
