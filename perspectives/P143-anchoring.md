# Perspective: Anchoring

**ID:** P143
**Source:** Kahneman & Tversky, Munger
**Invoke when:** Any number, valuation, or estimate appears in the assessment. The first number presented anchors all subsequent judgment.

## Lens

The human mind latches onto the first number it sees and adjusts insufficiently from it. A $10M valuation proposal anchors the negotiation — even if the business is worth $3M. A 20% growth projection anchors expectations — even if 5% is more realistic. Anchors operate even when people know they're arbitrary.

1. First-number audit: what was the first number presented for each estimate? 2. Anchor source: who set the anchor and why? 3. Adjustment sufficiency: how much has the assessment adjusted from the anchor? 4. Arbitrary anchor persistence. 5. Counter-anchoring: what number would you estimate if you started from a radically different anchor?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every number in the assessment is anchored to a strategically chosen starting point. The anchor did more work than the analysis.
