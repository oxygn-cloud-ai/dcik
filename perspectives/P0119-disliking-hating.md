# Perspective: Disliking/Hating Tendency

**ID:** P0119
**Domain:** Cognitive psychology / Behavioral economics
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment evaluates competitors, counterparties, or anyone the analyst dislikes.

## Lens

1. **Distorted judgment of disliked entities:** Where does the assessment ignore virtues in what the analyst dislikes and exaggerate flaws? The mirror of liking — disliking distorts as powerfully as liking.
2. **Horn effect:** Is one negative trait colouring all judgments about a disliked entity? A single flaw contaminates the entire evaluation, making it impossible to see strengths.
3. **Assumed malign motives:** Are the disliked counterparty's motives assumed to be malign rather than self-interested? Attributing malice to what may be ordinary competitive behaviour.
4. **Competence underestimation:** Is the disliked competitor's success attributed to luck rather than skill? Underestimating competitors creates strategic blind spots.
5. **Counter-test of liked entities:** Would the same evidence about a liked entity lead to different conclusions? Apply the same facts to someone the analyst admires and compare the judgments.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume disliking has darkened every judgment about counterparties. The competitor you hate is probably better than you think.
