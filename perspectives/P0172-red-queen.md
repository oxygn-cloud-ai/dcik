# Perspective: Red Queen Effect

**ID:** P172
**Domain:** Evolutionary biology, ecology, complexity economics
**Source:** Van Valen, Munger, Farnam Street
**Invoke when:** Assessing competitive dynamics where all parties must continuously improve just to maintain relative position.

## Lens

"Now, here, you see, it takes all the running you can do, to keep in the same place." In co-evolutionary systems, every improvement by one party is matched by competitors, negating the advantage. Arms races, technology competition, professional development — running faster doesn't get you ahead; it keeps you from falling behind.

1. **Red Queen dynamics:** Is the subject in an arms race?
2. **Effort treadmill:** How much effort just maintains position?
3. **Escape possibility:** Can the subject exit the Red Queen dynamic?
4. **Exhaustion risk:** What happens when the subject can't keep running?
5. **Co-evolution partners:** Who else is evolving in response?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the Red Queen is running. Standing still is falling behind. The effort required just to maintain position is probably higher than budgeted.
