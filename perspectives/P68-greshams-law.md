# Perspective: Gresham's Law Extended

**ID:** P68
**Domain:** Economics, information theory, organisational behaviour, sociology
**Source:** Gresham, Munger (extended application), information economics
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves environments where quality competes with quantity, truth competes with convenience, or standards compete with shortcuts.

## Lens

Gresham's original Law: "Bad money drives out good." When two forms of currency circulate and one is debased, people hoard the good and spend the bad — driving the good out of circulation. The law extends far beyond currency: bad information drives out good. Bad behaviour drives out good. Bad actors drive out good. Whenever quality can't be easily verified, the low-quality version wins.

1. **Quality degradation dynamics:** Where is quality being driven out? In the information environment (clickbait over journalism), in the organisation (politicians over performers), in the market (low-cost over high-quality), in relationships (transactional over trust-based).
2. **Verification cost:** Can quality be verified? If verification is costly or impossible before purchase (a "credence good"), low-quality providers will dominate. Lemons drive out peaches. Where is the verification gap?
3. **Race to the bottom:** Where is competitive pressure driving quality down? "Everyone else cuts corners, so we must too." This is Gresham's Law in action — the bad practice becomes the standard because the good practice can't be verified by customers.
4. **Gresham's reversal:** Under what conditions does good drive out bad? When quality IS verifiable (reviews, ratings, reputation systems), when regulation penalises bad actors, when consumers are educated, when the cost of being bad exceeds the benefit. Are these conditions present?
5. **Information Gresham:** In environments where engagement drives revenue, bad information (sensational, false, polarising) drives out good (accurate, nuanced, boring). Social media, news media, financial commentary. Is the assessment's information environment subject to Gresham dynamics?
6. **Organisational Gresham:** In organisations where politics determines advancement, politicians drive out performers. Where busyness is rewarded over output, busyness drives out productivity. Where activity is measured over results, activity drives out results.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume Gresham's Law is operating wherever quality can't be easily verified. Assume bad information, bad behaviour, and bad actors are driving out good ones. The environment that rewards bad over good will eventually contain only bad.
