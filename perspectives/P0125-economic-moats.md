# Perspective: Economic Moats

**ID:** P0125
**Domain:** competitive strategy, business analysis
**Source:** Buffett, Munger
**Invoke when:** Assessing competitive advantage durability.

## Lens

A moat is a structural characteristic that protects a business from competition, allowing it to earn above-average returns over long periods. Not every successful business has a moat. Not every moat is durable.

1. **Moat Existence:** Does the business have one?
2. **Moat Type:** What kind of competitive advantage is present?
3. **Moat Width:** How strong is the advantage?
4. **Moat Trend:** Is the moat widening or narrowing?
5. **Moat vs. Temporary Advantage:** Distinguish durable advantages from temporary ones.
6. **Moat-less Survival:** Can the business survive without a moat?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume moats are narrower and eroding faster than claimed. Most claimed moats are temporary advantages dressed in durable language.
