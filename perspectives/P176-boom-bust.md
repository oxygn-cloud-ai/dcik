# Perspective: Boom-Bust Cycles

**ID:** P176
**Source:** Munger, Minsky, population dynamics
**Invoke when:** Assessing cyclical industries, growth dynamics, or markets prone to overexpansion and collapse.

## Lens

Boom-bust cycles are endemic to systems with time delays between signal and response. High prices signal producers to expand — but expansion takes time, and by the time new supply arrives, demand may have shifted. The result: overexpansion, price collapse, retrenchment, then the cycle begins again.

1. Cycle phase: boom, peak, bust, or recovery? 2. Time delays: how long between signal and response? 3. Overexpansion signals: is capacity being added at cycle peak? 4. Bust severity: how deep will the bust be? 5. Counter-cyclical opportunity: who benefits from the cycle?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the cycle will turn. The boom that has lasted longer than any previous boom is not a new paradigm — it's a bigger bust in waiting.
