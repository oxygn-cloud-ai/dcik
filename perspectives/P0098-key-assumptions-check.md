# Perspective: Key Assumptions Check

**ID:** P0098
**Domain:** Intelligence analysis, methodology, epistemology
**Source:** Heuer & Pherson (Structured Analytic Techniques)
**Invoke when:** The assessment rests on assumptions — stated or unstated — that have not been explicitly identified and tested. Almost always.

## Lens

Every assessment is built on assumptions. Most are never stated, never examined, and never tested. A Key Assumptions Check systematically surfaces all assumptions and evaluates each for solidity. The assessment that cannot list its assumptions does not know what it depends on.

1. **Assumption extraction:** List every assumption the assessment depends on. Stated assumptions (explicitly noted). Unstated assumptions (baked into the analysis without acknowledgment). Structural assumptions (built into the methodology itself).
2. **Solidity rating:** Rate each assumption: Solid (well-supported by evidence), Correct with Caveats (generally true but with exceptions), Unsupported (no evidence or conflicting evidence), or Probably False (evidence contradicts but assumption retained).
3. **Key assumption identification:** Which assumptions, if wrong, would most change the conclusions? These are the key assumptions. The assessment's validity depends on them.
4. **Assumption stress testing:** For each key assumption: what evidence supports it? What evidence contradicts it? Under what conditions would it fail? How would the assessment change if it were false?
5. **Assumption interdependence:** Which assumptions depend on each other? If assumption A is false, does assumption B also become false? Cascade failures in assumptions are the most dangerous failures.
6. **Assumption monitoring:** For assumptions that cannot be verified now: what would confirm or refute them? When? How will this be monitored? Unmonitored assumptions are bets, not analysis.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume unstated assumptions are doing more analytical work than stated ones. Assume unsupported assumptions exist and have not been identified. The assessment that cannot list its key assumptions is guessing, not analysing.
