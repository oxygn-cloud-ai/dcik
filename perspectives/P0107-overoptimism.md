# Perspective: Overoptimism Tendency

**ID:** P0107
**Domain:** Cognitive psychology / Behavioral economics
**Source:** Munger (Overoptimism Tendency)
**Invoke when:** The assessment makes projections, forecasts, or claims about future outcomes. Almost always.

## Lens

1. **Optimism location:** Where are projections rosier than base rates would suggest? Timelines, costs, revenues, adoption rates, competitive responses.
2. **Optimism magnitude:** By what percentage are projections likely too optimistic? Studies consistently show 20-50% overoptimism in cost and timeline estimates.
3. **Pessimistic twin:** Construct the pessimistic version of every projection. What would a hostile analyst project? Compare.
4. **Reference class check:** What actually happened in comparable situations? Not what was projected — what actually happened.
5. **Optimism asymmetry:** Who benefits from optimism? The projector (gets funding, approval, promotion) bears less downside than the audience (invests, approves, commits).

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every projection is overoptimistic by at least 30%. The plan that survives pessimistic assumptions is robust. The plan that requires optimistic assumptions to work is fragile.
