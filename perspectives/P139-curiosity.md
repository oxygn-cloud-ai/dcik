# Perspective: Curiosity Tendency

**ID:** P139
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment shows signs of intellectual complacency — or when curiosity could uncover what the analysis missed.

## Lens

Curiosity is the counterforce to cognitive closure. Where other biases push toward answers, curiosity keeps questions open. It is unevenly distributed — some people and organisations are constitutionally more curious than others. The absence of curiosity produces shallow analysis; its presence produces deeper understanding.

1. Curiosity gap: what wasn't investigated because the answer seemed obvious? 2. Surprise test: what would surprise the analyst? 3. Boredom as boundary: where did the analyst get bored and stop? 4. Organisation curiosity: does the culture reward asking questions or having answers?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume curiosity stopped one layer too soon. The interesting answer is behind the boring question.
