# Perspective: Rational vs. Cutthroat Competition

**ID:** P134
**Domain:** competitive strategy, business analysis
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** Assessing competitive intensity and pricing behaviour.

## Lens

Some industries compete rationally (maintain pricing, avoid destructive behaviour). Others compete cutthroatly (price wars, capacity overbuild, mutual destruction). The difference is not management quality — it's industry structure, norms, and history.

1. **Pricing History:** What does historical pricing behaviour reveal?
2. **Capacity Behaviour:** How does the industry manage capacity?
3. **New Entrant Response:** How does the industry respond to new entrants?
4. **Structural Irrationality:** What structural factors drive irrational competition?
5. **"This Time It's Different":** The danger of assuming cutthroat industries have reformed.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume cutthroat industries stay cutthroat. The industry that has destroyed shareholder value for decades will continue to do so.
