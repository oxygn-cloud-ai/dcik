# Perspective: Cognitive Biases Extended

**ID:** P0027
**Domain:** Psychology, behavioural economics, cognitive science
**Source:** Munger (25 tendencies), Kahneman, Cialdini, Farnam Street
**Compendium:** true
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves human judgment, predictions, negotiations, or decision-making. Always — bias is universal.

> **Note:** This is a compendium perspective — it catalogs multiple related biases in a single reference file. The standard 12-question cap is waived for compendiums.

## Lens

P15 covers general cognitive bias awareness. This perspective extends it with Munger's specific psychological tendencies and social-proof dynamics. Apply systematically — not "is there bias?" but "which of these specific biases are operating?"

### Individual Biases

1. **Overoptimism:** Are projections systematically rosy? What would the pessimistic version look like? Why isn't that the base case?
2. **Doubt-Avoidance:** Is the assessment rushing to closure? Are uncomfortable uncertainties being resolved by fiat rather than investigation?
3. **Inconsistency-Avoidance:** Is the assessment defending prior commitments rather than updating? "We've always said X, therefore X must be true."
4. **First-Conclusion Bias:** Was the first answer accepted without testing alternatives? What was the second-best answer?
5. **Excessive Self-Regard:** Are the analyst's own capabilities (or the subject's) overestimated? What would an independent evaluator conclude?
6. **Deprival-Superreaction:** Is loss aversion distorting the analysis? Are sunk costs being protected rather than evaluated?
7. **Contrast-Misreaction:** Are judgments being made relative to an irrelevant anchor rather than absolute value?
8. **Availability-Misweighing:** Are recent or vivid examples overweighted relative to their statistical frequency?
9. **Authority-Misinfluence:** Is authority being substituted for evidence? "Expert X says" is not analysis.
10. **Reason-Respecting:** Are explanations accepted because they sound plausible, not because they're supported?

### Social Biases

11. **Social-Proof:** Are conclusions influenced by what others believe? Would the analysis differ if the consensus were different?
12. **Liking/Loving:** Is favourable treatment being given to people or ideas the analyst likes? Would the same evidence from a disliked source receive the same weight?
13. **Disliking/Hating:** Is unfavourable treatment being given to people or ideas the analyst dislikes?
14. **Envy/Jealousy:** Is the analysis shaped by what others have that the subject doesn't?
15. **Influence-from-Mere-Association:** Is a past success or failure (by association) colouring judgment of an unrelated situation?
16. **Reciprocation:** Is the analyst reciprocating — consciously or not — for past favours, access, or relationship?
17. **Kantian Fairness:** Is fairness being demanded in a way that distorts the analysis? Is the "fair" outcome different from the accurate outcome?

### Structural Biases

18. **Man-with-a-Hammer:** Is one analytical tool being applied to everything? "To a hammer, everything looks like a nail."
19. **Twaddle:** Is the assessment longer than it needs to be? Is verbosity hiding weak analysis?
20. **Stress-Influence:** Is stress (deadline pressure, stakes, fatigue) degrading the quality of the analysis?
21. **Simple, Pain-Avoiding Denial:** Is an uncomfortable reality being ignored because it's too painful to face?
22. **Status Quo Bias:** Is the default option being favoured? What would the analysis conclude if the status quo weren't an option?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every one of these biases is operating to some degree. The analyst who says "I'm not biased" is demonstrating Excessive Self-Regard. The question is not whether bias exists, but which biases are operating and how much they distort the conclusions.
