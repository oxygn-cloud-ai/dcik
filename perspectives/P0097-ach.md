# Perspective: Analysis of Competing Hypotheses

**ID:** P0097
**Domain:** Intelligence analysis, epistemology, methodology
**Source:** Richards Heuer (Psychology of Intelligence Analysis)
**Invoke when:** The assessment evaluates evidence against multiple possible explanations — or should but doesn't.

## Lens

ACH is the gold standard for evaluating evidence against competing hypotheses. Instead of building a case for the preferred explanation (which confirmation bias makes inevitable), ACH starts with all plausible hypotheses and evaluates each piece of evidence against each hypothesis. The hypothesis most consistent with the evidence — not most favoured by the analyst — survives.

1. **Hypothesis enumeration:** List all plausible hypotheses, including "something we haven't thought of." The preferred hypothesis is just one among many at this stage.
2. **Evidence matrix:** For each piece of evidence, rate consistency with each hypothesis (consistent, inconsistent, neutral, not applicable). Evidence that is consistent with all hypotheses is diagnostically worthless.
3. **Diagnostic evidence focus:** Which evidence best discriminates between hypotheses? Focus on evidence inconsistent with the leading hypothesis — this is what confirmation bias would ignore.
4. **Refutation, not confirmation:** The goal is to eliminate hypotheses, not confirm the favourite. The surviving hypothesis is the one least inconsistent with the evidence — not the one most supported.
5. **Sensitivity analysis:** How sensitive are the conclusions to key pieces of evidence? If a single piece of evidence would change the ranking, how reliable is that evidence?
6. **Deception consideration:** Could the evidence have been planted, manipulated, or selectively presented? In adversarial contexts, evidence is weaponised.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the assessment has built a case for the preferred hypothesis rather than testing all hypotheses. Assume diagnostic evidence has been underweighted. The hypothesis that survives ACH is rarely the one the analyst started with.
