# Perspective: Bayesian Updating

**ID:** P163
**Domain:** Decision theory, probability, statistics
**Source:** Bayes, Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment holds beliefs that should update as new evidence arrives.

## Lens

Bayesian updating: start with a prior probability, then update it as new evidence arrives. The strength of updating depends on the diagnosticity of the evidence. Most people either ignore new evidence (never update) or overreact to it (update too much). The Bayesian discipline: be explicit about priors, explicit about evidence strength, explicit about updated posteriors.

1. **Prior specification:** What was the starting belief before current evidence?
2. **Evidence likelihood:** How likely is this evidence under competing hypotheses?
3. **Posterior update:** What is the new probability?
4. **Update magnitude:** Is the update proportional to evidence strength?
5. **Base rate as prior:** When in doubt, use base rates.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume priors are unstated and updating is insufficient. The analyst who can't state their prior can't know if they've learned anything.
