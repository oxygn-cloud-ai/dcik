# Perspective: Twaddle Tendency

**ID:** P140
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment uses more words than necessary, substitutes length for rigour, or buries weak analysis in volume.

## Lens

Twaddle is verbal bloat — words that fill space without adding information. It is produced by people who are paid by the word, measured by output volume, or incentivised to appear productive rather than be productive. Long assessments are not deep assessments. Brevity is a signal of clarity.

1. Length audit: could this be half as long? A quarter? 2. Bloat location: where does the assessment say in 10 words what could be said in 3? 3. Twaddle as hiding: what weak point is being buried in verbosity? 4. Incentive check: does the system reward length or clarity?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume length is inversely correlated with rigour. The 3-page memo that took 3 weeks to write is worth more than the 30-page report that took 3 days.
