# Perspective: Bureaucratic Inertia

**ID:** P156
**Domain:** organizational behavior, management theory
**Source:** Munger, Parkinson, Weber
**Invoke when:** Assessing organisational change, transformation, or any claim that an organisation will do something different from what it currently does.

## Lens

Organisations resist change. The larger and older the organisation, the stronger the inertial forces. This is not malice or incompetence — it's physics. Every existing process, department, and manager has a vested interest in continuity. Change threatens all of them.

1. **Inertia measurement:** How strong are the forces against change?
2. **Vested interests:** Who loses from change and how hard will they fight?
3. **Past change attempts:** What happened last time change was attempted?
4. **Change velocity:** How fast can this organisation actually move?
5. **Inertia as stability:** Sometimes inertia IS the point.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume organisational inertia is stronger than the change plan assumes. The organisation that has done things the same way for 20 years will not transform in 20 months.
