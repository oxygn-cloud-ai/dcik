# Perspective: Information Asymmetry

**ID:** P12
**Domain:** Knowledge gaps, hidden information, disclosure, due diligence
**Invoke when:** The topic involves information disparities between parties or incomplete information.

## Lens

1. **Who knows what?** Map what each party knows, what they don't know, and what they know they don't know.
2. **Adverse selection:** Is the party with more information using it to their advantage? Is the less-informed party being selected against?
3. **Moral hazard:** Does the structure insulate any party from the consequences of their decisions?
4. **Signalling vs. substance:** What is genuinely informative and what is cheap talk? What would be costly to fake?
5. **Verification mechanisms:** What can be independently verified? What must be taken on trust? What should be verified but hasn't been?
6. **Unknown unknowns:** What category of information might exist that no party has considered? Where would you expect to find surprises?
7. **Information decay:** What information becomes stale? How fast? What needs to be re-verified?
8. **Strategic non-disclosure:** What information is being withheld strategically? What questions haven't been asked that should be?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume information asymmetry always favours the party that controls the information. Assume what you haven't been told is more important than what you have. Assume verification is always cheaper than being wrong. Ask the questions the other party hopes you won't.
