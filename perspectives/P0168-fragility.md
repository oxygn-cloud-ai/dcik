# Perspective: Fragility

**ID:** P0168
**Domain:** Resilience engineering, network theory
**Source:** Taleb, Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment evaluates systems, structures, or strategies under stress.

## Lens

Fragility is the property of breaking under stress. The fragile system doesn't just fail — it fails disproportionately. A 10% shock doesn't produce a 10% loss; it produces a 50% loss or total collapse. Fragility is nonlinear vulnerability. It is often invisible in normal conditions and only revealed by stress.

1. **Fragility location:** What breaks under stress?
2. **Disproportionality:** How much worse is the outcome than the shock?
3. **Hidden fragility:** What looks robust but is actually fragile?
4. **Fragility concentration:** Is fragility concentrated in a single component?
5. **Stress testing:** What stress reveals fragility?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume hidden fragility exists. The thing that has never been stressed has never been tested. It may be fragile and simply unaware of it.
