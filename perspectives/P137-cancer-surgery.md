# Perspective: Cancer-Surgery Formula

**ID:** P137
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment avoids painful but necessary excision — cutting a division, firing a leader, abandoning a strategy.

## Lens

Sometimes the only way to save the organism is to cut out the diseased part. Killing 5% of the business may be the only way to save the other 95%. The metaphor is surgical: delayed excision lets the cancer metastasise. Most assessments avoid recommending excision because it's painful, visible, and carries career risk for the recommender.

1. What needs to be cut? 2. Why hasn't it been cut? 3. Metastasis risk: what happens if cutting is delayed? 4. Post-excision health: what does the organism look like after? 5. Who loses from excision and who gains? The losers scream louder.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume necessary excision is being avoided. The recommendation to cut something is almost always right and almost always resisted.
