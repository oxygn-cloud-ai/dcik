# Perspective: Nonlinearity

**ID:** P165
**Source:** Systems theory, Taleb, Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment assumes proportional relationships — double the input, double the output.

## Lens

In nonlinear systems, small inputs can produce disproportionately large outputs — and large inputs can produce surprisingly small outputs. The dose makes the poison. The last straw breaks the camel's back. Most real-world systems are nonlinear; most models assume linearity.

1. Nonlinearity location: where are small changes producing large effects or large changes producing small effects? 2. Threshold effects: where are the cliffs? 3. Diminishing returns: where does more input produce less additional output? 4. Amplification: where do small inputs cascade? 5. Linear assumption audit.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume nonlinearity everywhere. The linear model is the wrong model. Small changes at critical points produce outsized outcomes.
