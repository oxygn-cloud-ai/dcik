# Perspective: Setting & Context

**ID:** P77
**Domain:** Systems, strategy, psychology, communication
**Source:** Farnam Street (Art section — Setting), ecological psychology, context effects
**Invoke when:** The assessment evaluates behaviour, performance, or outcomes without accounting for the environment in which they occur.

## Lens

Behaviour is a function of the person and the situation — and the situation usually dominates. The same person in a different environment behaves differently. The same strategy in a different market produces different results. Context is not background — it is causal.

1. **Environment audit:** What is the physical, temporal, social, regulatory, and competitive environment? For each: how does it enable or constrain the behaviour being analysed?
2. **Context-dependence:** Which conclusions are context-dependent? Would they hold in a different market, era, regulatory regime, or competitive landscape? If yes: the conclusion is about the context, not the subject.
3. **Environment change:** How is the environment changing? What behaviours will the new environment enable or constrain? The strategy that works in the current environment may fail in the next.
4. **Person-situation attribution error:** Is the assessment attributing to the person (skill, strategy, character) what should be attributed to the situation (luck, timing, environment)? This is the fundamental attribution error at scale.
5. **Context design:** Can the environment be changed to produce different behaviour? Changing incentives, defaults, physical layout, social norms. Context design is often cheaper and more effective than trying to change people.
6. **Historical context blindness:** Is the assessment judging past decisions without accounting for what was known at the time? "They should have seen it coming" — but did they have the information, tools, and frameworks we have now?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume context explains more than the assessment credits. Assume person-focused explanations are inflated. Change the context and you change the behaviour — the person was always responding to their environment.
