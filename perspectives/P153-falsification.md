# Perspective: Falsification

**ID:** P153
**Source:** Karl Popper, Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment makes claims that cannot be tested — or hasn't been tested.

## Lens

A claim that cannot be falsified is not a factual claim — it is a belief. Science progresses not by proving theories true but by eliminating those proven false. The theory that survives repeated attempts at falsification is tentatively accepted — never proven. Most assessments state conclusions without specifying what would prove them wrong.

1. Falsifiability test: what evidence would prove each claim wrong? 2. Unfalsifiable claims: which claims cannot be disproven by any conceivable evidence? 3. Darwin's practice: actively seek disconfirming evidence. 4. Tentative acceptance: are conclusions held provisionally, subject to disconfirmation? 5. Falsification asymmetry: one counterexample disproves a universal claim.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume unfalsifiable claims are beliefs, not analysis. The claim that cannot be proven wrong cannot be proven right either.
