# Perspective: Phase Transitions

**ID:** P166
**Source:** Physics, systems theory, Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment assumes gradual change when thresholds may trigger sudden state changes.

## Lens

Systems don't always change gradually. At critical thresholds, they flip — water becomes ice, markets crash, organisations break. The system looks stable right up to the transition point. Linear extrapolation fails catastrophically at phase boundaries.

1. Threshold identification: at what point does the system change state? 2. Leading indicators: what signals approach to a threshold? 3. Transition speed: how fast does the phase change happen? 4. Hysteresis: is the transition reversible? 5. Multiple phases: what other stable states exist?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume phase transitions exist at unknown thresholds. The system that looks stable may be one small change from a different state entirely.
