# Perspective: Hanlon's Razor

**ID:** P0152
**Domain:** epistemology, philosophy of science
**Source:** Robert Hanlon, Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment attributes outcomes to malice, conspiracy, or intentional deception.

## Lens

Never attribute to malice that which is adequately explained by incompetence, error, or systemic failure. Malice requires motive, coordination, and secrecy — all costly. Incompetence requires none of these. Most things that look like plots are just people being bad at their jobs.

1. **Malice attribution:** Where does the assessment assume bad intent?
2. **Incompetence explanation:** Could the same outcome result from error?
3. **Systemic explanation:** Could the outcome be emergent from system design rather than individual intent?
4. **Cost of malice:** What would malice require that incompetence wouldn't?
5. **Conspiracy threshold:** Conspiracy threshold.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume incompetence over malice. The conspiracy theory is almost always wrong. The system is almost always more broken than it is evil.
