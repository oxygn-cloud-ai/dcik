# Perspective: Operational & Execution

**ID:** P0014
**Domain:** Implementation, operations, execution risk, practical delivery
**Invoke when:** The topic requires practical implementation, has operational dimensions, or depends on execution quality.

## Lens

1. **Who does the work?** Identify every person and team needed. Do they exist? Are they available? Do they have the skills?
2. **Execution timeline:** Map every step from decision to outcome. Where are the bottlenecks? What is the critical path?
3. **Resource requirements:** What is needed beyond capital? Talent, relationships, permits, infrastructure, time?
4. **Key person risk:** What happens if any critical person leaves, becomes unavailable, or underperforms?
5. **Operational failure modes:** What are the most common operational failures for this type of endeavour? What percentage fail operationally vs. strategically?
6. **Coordination complexity:** How many parties need to coordinate? What is the communication overhead? Where do coordination failures typically occur?
7. **Quality control:** How is quality measured and maintained? What happens when quality slips?
8. **Institutional capacity:** Does the executing entity have the institutional muscle to deliver? Past track record of similar execution?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume execution is where good ideas go to die. Assume key people leave. Assume timelines slip by 2x. Assume coordination failures at every handoff. Assume the plan looks nothing like reality by month 3. Execution risk kills more good ideas than bad ideas do.
