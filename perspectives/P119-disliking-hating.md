# Perspective: Disliking/Hating Tendency

**ID:** P119
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment evaluates competitors, counterparties, or anyone the analyst dislikes.

## Lens

The mirror of P118. Humans ignore virtues in what they dislike and exaggerate flaws. The disliked competitor's success is attributed to luck. The disliked counterparty's motives are assumed malign. Disliking distorts as powerfully as liking.

1. Distorted judgment of disliked entities. 2. Horn effect (one negative trait colours everything). 3. Assumed malign motives. 4. Competitor competence underestimated. 5. Counter-test: same evidence about a liked entity.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume disliking has darkened every judgment about counterparties. The competitor you hate is probably better than you think.
