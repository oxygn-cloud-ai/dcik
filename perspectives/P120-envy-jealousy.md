# Perspective: Envy/Jealousy Tendency

**ID:** P120
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves comparisons between parties, relative outcomes, or competitive dynamics.

## Lens

Envy is the most denied human emotion — and one of the most powerful drivers of behaviour. People will sacrifice absolute welfare to avoid being worse off than their peers. Envy drives competitive escalation, status consumption, and irrational opposition to others' success.

1. Envy-driven behaviour in markets, organisations, negotiations. 2. "Fairness" claims that are actually envy. 3. Relative vs. absolute welfare preferences. 4. Envy denial detection.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume envy is driving behaviour that is rationalised as something else. "Fairness" often means "I want what they have."
