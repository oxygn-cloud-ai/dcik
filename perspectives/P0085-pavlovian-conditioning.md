# Perspective: Pavlovian Conditioning

**ID:** P85
**Domain:** Psychology, behavioural conditioning, learning
**Source:** Pavlov, Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves repeated patterns, habits, or automatic responses — in markets, organisations, or individuals.

## Lens

Pavlov's dogs salivated at the sound of a bell because the bell predicted food. The association became automatic. Humans are no different: we develop automatic responses to stimuli that predict outcomes — often without awareness. Markets rally on Fed statements. Employees flinch at "restructuring." Investors chase the asset class that went up last year.

1. **Conditioned response mapping:** What stimuli trigger automatic responses? Market signals, management phrases, competitor actions, regulatory announcements. What is the conditioned response?
2. **Conditioning source:** How was the association formed? Repeated pairing, intense single experience, social transmission. Is the association valid (the stimulus still predicts the outcome) or outdated (the world changed but the response remains)?
3. **Extinction:** Has the conditioning been extinguished? If the stimulus no longer predicts the outcome, how long until the response fades? Extinction takes longer than acquisition — old associations persist long after they stop being valid.
4. **Generalisation:** Has the response generalised to similar stimuli? After one bad experience with a type of investment, partner, or strategy, avoidance generalises to the entire category — even when the new instance is different.
5. **Counter-conditioning:** Can a new association replace the old one? This is harder than it sounds. The old response is automatic; the new one requires conscious effort.
6. **Exploitation of conditioning:** Is anyone deliberately conditioning responses? Marketing conditions brand loyalty. Management conditions employee behaviour through rewards. Regulators condition market responses through policy signals.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume conditioned responses are operating below awareness. Assume old associations persist after they stop being valid. The market that rallies on a Fed statement is Pavlov's dog — the bell rings, the response follows, and no one stops to ask whether food is actually coming.
