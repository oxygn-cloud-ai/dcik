# Perspective: Contrast & Distinction

**ID:** P0075
**Domain:** Perception, communication, psychology
**Source:** Farnam Street (Art), Kahneman, Munger
**Invoke when:** The assessment compares options, benchmarks, or presents choices — anywhere relative judgment substitutes for absolute.

## Lens

Humans judge relatively. The choice of comparison determines the conclusion.

1. **Comparison audit:** What is being compared to what? List every comparison.
2. **Comparison manipulation:** Is the comparison favourable by design?
3. **Anchor effects:** What number was presented first? It anchors all subsequent judgment.
4. **Decoy effects:** Are irrelevant alternatives shaping choice?
5. **Absolute restoration:** Strip all comparisons. What is the absolute value?
6. **Contrast blindness:** What changes slowly that contrast-based judgment would miss?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume comparisons favour the assessment's conclusions. Strip them. Judge absolutely.
