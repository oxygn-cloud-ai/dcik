# Perspective: Surfing Long Waves

**ID:** P0136
**Domain:** competitive strategy, business analysis
**Source:** Munger
**Invoke when:** Assessing whether success is driven by secular trends rather than company-specific factors.

## Lens

Many successful companies are surfing powerful secular trends — demographic shifts, technological change, regulatory evolution. When the wave is strong, even average companies look brilliant. When the wave recedes, even brilliant companies struggle.

1. **Wave Identification:** What secular trend is the subject riding?
2. **Wave Attribution:** Is success from surfing or swimming?
3. **Wave Phase:** Is the wave building, cresting, or receding?
4. **Wave-Dependence Risk:** How dependent is the business on the wave?
5. **Swimming Capability:** Can the business survive if the wave disappears?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume success is more attributable to the wave than the surfer. The brilliant CEO in a booming market is the lucky CEO in a random walk.
