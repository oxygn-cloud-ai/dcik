# Perspective: Velocity & Direction

**ID:** P42
**Domain:** Strategy, physics metaphor, decision-making, measurement
**Source:** Farnam Street (Physics section), business strategy
**Invoke when:** The assessment measures progress, growth, or performance. Any claim about "moving forward" or "growing."

## Lens

Speed is how fast you're moving. Velocity is speed with direction. Most assessments measure speed and call it progress. Moving fast in the wrong direction is worse than standing still.

1. **Speed vs. velocity audit:** What metrics measure speed? What metrics measure direction? Revenue growth is speed. Revenue growth toward a sustainable market position is velocity. Are you measuring both?
2. **Directional drift:** Is the direction changing without acknowledgment? Organisations drift. Strategies drift. The original direction and the current trajectory may differ. When was direction last explicitly confirmed?
3. **Is the direction right?** Independent of speed: is the destination worth reaching? A fast-growing business heading toward a dying market has high speed and terrible velocity.
4. **Acceleration and deceleration:** Is speed increasing or decreasing? Is the rate of change itself changing (jerk)? Second and third derivatives matter — a decelerating growth rate may still be growing but the trend is negative.
5. **Friction and drag:** What is slowing progress? Organisational friction, regulatory drag, competitive resistance, market saturation. Reducing friction often yields more progress than increasing thrust.
6. **Course correction cost:** How expensive is it to change direction? The faster you're moving, the larger the turning radius. High-speed entities cannot pivot — they can only crash or continue.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume speed is being measured because it's easy, while direction is being ignored because it's hard. Assume growth for growth's sake. The entity moving slowly in the right direction will eventually overtake the one sprinting in the wrong direction.
