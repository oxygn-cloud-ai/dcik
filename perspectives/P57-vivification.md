# Perspective: Vivification

**ID:** P57
**Domain:** Communication, persuasion, memory, decision psychology
**Source:** Munger (Vivification)
**Invoke when:** The assessment uses abstract language, statistics, or generalities where concrete, vivid examples would clarify — or where the abstract framing may be concealing weak evidence.

## Lens

Abstract ideas are forgettable. Vivid images are memorable. "A million deaths" is a statistic. "A single dead child" is a tragedy. Vivification is the deliberate translation of abstractions into concrete, visceral terms — not to manipulate, but to ensure the true weight of the analysis is felt, not just calculated.

1. **Abstraction audit:** Where does the assessment use abstractions that hide the human or practical reality? "Downside risk of $X" — what does losing $X actually mean? "Market dislocation" — what does that look like for a real person?
2. **Vivify the downside:** Translate the worst-case scenario into concrete terms. Not "50% loss of capital" but "you cannot make payroll, your employees lose their jobs, your investors lose confidence, your reputation is damaged." Make it real.
3. **Vivify the upside:** Translate the best case. Not "3x return" but "you can fund your next venture, your early employees buy houses, your angel investors get their best return." What does success actually look like?
4. **Vivification vs. manipulation:** Is vivid language being used to manipulate (making the unlikely seem probable through emotional force) or to clarify (making the abstract concrete so it can be evaluated honestly)? Distinguish them.
5. **The vividness heuristic:** People overweight vivid, easily-imagined outcomes and underweight abstract, statistical ones. Is the assessment exploiting this (making the preferred outcome more vivid) or correcting for it (vivifying the less vivid but more probable outcomes)?
6. **One vivid example:** If the entire assessment had to be communicated through a single vivid example, what would it be? If you can't produce one, the analysis may be too abstract to be actionable.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume abstract language conceals weak evidence. Vivify the worst case to ensure it's genuinely being confronted, not just mathematically acknowledged. The analysis that can't be made vivid hasn't been fully understood.
