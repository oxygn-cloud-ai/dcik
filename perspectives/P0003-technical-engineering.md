# Perspective: Technical & Engineering

**ID:** P03
**Domain:** Technology, systems, implementation, architecture, feasibility
**Invoke when:** The topic involves technical systems, engineering decisions, or technological feasibility.

## Lens

1. **Feasibility:** Is it actually possible? At what scale? Reliability? Prerequisites?
2. **Complexity depth:** Interacting systems? Failure modes? Single points of failure?
3. **Dependency chain:** Third-party dependencies outside principal's control?
4. **Security posture:** Threat model? Attack surfaces? Data exposure?
5. **Scalability limits:** Where does it break under load? Cost curve per order-of-magnitude?
6. **Technical debt:** Shortcuts that cost more later? What is being hand-waved?
7. **Obsolescence risk:** End-of-life technologies in stack? Emerging replacements?
8. **Build vs. buy vs. depend:** Right choice for each component? Critical dependency failure?

## Default adversarial stance

Every technical claim is optimistic. Systems fail in unanticipated ways. Dependencies have outages, price increases, breaking changes. Security vulnerabilities exist and will be found.
