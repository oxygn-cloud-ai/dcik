# Perspective: Activation Energy & Catalysts

**ID:** P58
**Domain:** Chemistry metaphor, strategy, change management, psychology
**Source:** Farnam Street (Chemistry section)
**Invoke when:** The assessment assumes change will happen, progress will be made, or goals will be achieved — without examining the energy required to start and the catalysts that might reduce that energy.

## Lens

Every transformation requires an initial burst of energy — activation energy — to overcome inertia and begin. Once started, the reaction may be self-sustaining (exothermic). But without that initial push, nothing happens. Catalysts lower the activation energy without being consumed in the process.

1. **Activation energy audit:** For each planned change, initiative, or transformation: what is the activation energy? The initial capital, decision, approval, hire, contract, or commitment required to move from "planning" to "doing." How much? From where?
2. **Initiation barrier height:** Which initiatives have the highest activation energy? These are the ones most likely to stall in "planning" indefinitely. The higher the barrier, the more deliberate the commitment must be — or the initiative should be redesigned to lower the barrier.
3. **Catalyst identification:** What catalysts exist or could be introduced? A catalyst accelerates change without being consumed: a champion with authority, a demonstration project, a competitive threat, a regulatory change, a new technology, a forcing function. Which are available?
4. **Catalyst vs. fuel confusion:** A catalyst starts the reaction. Fuel sustains it. Many initiatives confuse the two — they budget for fuel (ongoing resources) but have no catalyst (the thing that actually gets it started). Where is this confusion operating?
5. **False starts:** Where has activation energy been applied but the reaction didn't sustain? Failed initiatives, abandoned projects, restructured departments. What was missing — insufficient activation energy, absence of catalyst, or lack of sustaining fuel?
6. **Exothermic vs. endothermic:** Once started, does the reaction generate its own energy (momentum, enthusiasm, results that attract more resources) or does it require continuous energy input? Exothermic initiatives are self-sustaining; endothermic ones eventually exhaust their sponsors.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume activation energy is underestimated. Assume initiatives will stall before starting without deliberate catalyst design. The gap between "we should" and "we did" is activation energy. Most things that should happen don't — not because they're bad ideas, but because no one supplied the initial push.
