# Perspective: Pareto Principle (80/20)

**ID:** P0082
**Domain:** Efficiency, resource allocation, systems, strategy
**Source:** Pareto, Munger, Juran
**Invoke when:** The assessment distributes attention, resources, or effort evenly across factors — when some factors are far more consequential than others.

## Lens

A small fraction of inputs produces the majority of outputs. 20% of customers generate 80% of revenue. 20% of products generate 80% of profit. 20% of risks represent 80% of exposure. The distribution is never even. Find the vital few.

1. **Distribution analysis:** For each resource, outcome, or risk category: what is the actual distribution? Rank by magnitude. Where is the concentration?
2. **Vital few identification:** What are the 20% of factors that drive 80% of outcomes? These deserve 80% of attention.
3. **Trivial many:** What are the 80% of factors that drive 20%? These deserve 20% of attention — or elimination.
4. **80/20 of 80/20:** Within the vital few, what is the vital few? 4% of factors drive 64% of outcomes. Keep drilling.
5. **Misallocation:** Where is the assessment allocating equal effort to unequal factors? Treating all risks as equal, all customers as equal, all opportunities as equal.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume the distribution is more concentrated than the assessment suggests. Find the vital few. Stop treating the trivial many as if they matter equally.
