# Perspective: Competition & Moat Dynamics

**ID:** P29
**Domain:** Competitive strategy, industry analysis, market structure
**Source:** Porter, Buffett/Munger (moats), Schumpeter (creative destruction), Christensen (disruption)
**Invoke when:** The assessment involves business strategy, competitive positioning, market entry, or durability of advantages.

## Lens

Every business exists in a competitive ecosystem. Advantages erode. Moats fill in. The only question is how fast. Analyse the competitive dynamics with the assumption that competitors are actively trying to destroy the subject's position.

1. **Moat inventory:** What are the claimed competitive advantages? List every one. For each: cost advantages, switching costs, network effects, brand power, regulatory protection, scale economies, data advantages, distribution lock-in, intellectual property.
2. **Moat depth:** For each claimed moat: how deep is it? How long would it take a well-funded competitor to replicate it? What would it cost?
3. **Moat erosion rate:** How fast is each advantage eroding? Technology changes, regulatory shifts, customer behaviour evolution, competitor investment — what is the half-life of each moat?
4. **Industry structure:** What is the market form? Monopoly, oligopoly, monopolistic competition, perfect competition? What are the structural forces (Porter's five) shaping profitability?
5. **Creative destruction:** What innovation would make the subject's entire business model obsolete? When did that last happen to a comparable business? What is the probability it happens in the relevant time horizon?
6. **Winner-take-all dynamics:** Does this market tend toward concentration or fragmentation? Why? What are the tipping points?
7. **Competitor incentives:** What do competitors want? How badly do they want this market? What are their resources? What would they sacrifice to win?
8. **Rational vs. cutthroat competition:** Is the industry disciplined (competitors maintain pricing) or self-destructive (price wars, capacity overbuild)? What determines which?

## Default adversarial stance

Assume every competitive advantage erodes faster than the assessment claims. Assume competitors are smarter, better-funded, and more motivated than they appear. Assume creative destruction arrives sooner than expected. The question is not whether moats exist — it's whether they're deep enough and durable enough to matter over the investment horizon.
