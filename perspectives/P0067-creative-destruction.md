# Perspective: Creative Destruction

**ID:** P67
**Domain:** Innovation, strategy, economics, competition
**Source:** Schumpeter, Munger, Christensen (disruption)
**Invoke when:** The assessment assumes existing advantages, business models, or technologies will persist. When it doesn't account for the replacement of old by new.

## Lens

Creative destruction is the process by which innovation destroys existing structures and creates new ones. The smartphone destroyed the camera, GPS, and alarm clock industries. Streaming destroyed physical media. AI will destroy... something. The pattern is inevitable. The only questions are what, when, and who.

1. **Destruction vulnerability:** What could destroy the subject's business model? Not "what competitor could take share" — what could make the entire model obsolete? Technology, regulation, consumer behaviour, demographic change, environmental constraint. List every existential threat.
2. **Incumbent trap:** Why won't the incumbent respond? They have resources, relationships, brand, data. The answer is usually: because responding would cannibalise their existing business. The innovator's dilemma is real — incumbents are structurally incapable of responding to disruption until it's too late.
3. **Destruction timeline:** How fast is the destruction coming? Creative destruction is not linear — it's slow, slow, slow, then suddenly everything changes. Where on the S-curve is the disruptive force?
4. **Destruction source:** Where will destruction come from? Startups (agile, unencumbered), adjacent industries (different cost structures, different assumptions), technological convergence (two existing technologies combining to create something new), regulatory change (government-mandated obsolescence).
5. **Creative side:** What new value is being created by the destruction? Creative destruction is not just destruction — it creates new industries, new jobs, new capabilities. What is being born as the old dies?
6. **Surfing destruction:** Can the assessment's subject surf the wave rather than be crushed by it? Incumbents who embrace creative destruction (Netflix killing its own DVD business with streaming) survive. Those who resist (Blockbuster) die.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume creative destruction is coming faster than the assessment acknowledges. Assume incumbents will not respond effectively. Assume the source of destruction is not on the competitive radar. The question is not whether existing structures will be destroyed — it's whether the assessment's subject will be the destroyer or the destroyed.
