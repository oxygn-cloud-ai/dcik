# Perspective: Culture as Control System

**ID:** P155
**Source:** Munger, organisational theory
**Invoke when:** Assessing organisational behaviour, strategy execution, or cultural claims.

## Lens

Culture controls behaviour more powerfully than rules, incentives, or strategy. "Culture eats strategy for breakfast." What the culture rewards gets done. What it punishes gets hidden. What it ignores withers. Culture is not what the values statement says — it's what happens when no one is watching.

1. Actual vs. stated culture. 2. Reward alignment: what does the culture actually reward? 3. Punishment alignment: what does it punish? 4. Founder imprint: how much of the culture IS the founder? 5. Culture change feasibility.

## Default adversarial stance

Assume stated culture and actual culture diverge. The values on the wall are aspirational. The behaviour in the corridor is real.
